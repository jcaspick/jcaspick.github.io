About

I’m a programmer, technical artist, and general computer graphics nerd. My driving passion in life is to never stop learning. I’m the sort of person who cycles through many different hobbies/interests/obsessions, but the ones I’ve consistently come back to throughout my life are computer graphics and programming. I’ve been exploring the twin worlds of graphics and code ever since I was a kid using ResEdit and a primitive 3D program called Mechanisto to add my own ships to the classic Mac game Escape Velocity.

I began my career as a 3D artist in the training and simulation industry. I had been learning how to code in my spare time, doing hobby projects in Unity 3D, and experimenting with VR headsets. I was able to find ways of applying these new skills at work and eventually grew into my current niche as a technical artist, a job that nicely accommodates my interest in both software and graphics. A lot of my work is on so-called “serious game design.” I use the same tools and technologies as a video game developer for the purposes of training and education. Another major part of my job is developing custom tools to solve project-specific problems, improve workflows, and help the 3D artists on my team be more productive and less miserable by automating tedious tasks.

On this website you’ll find blog posts about whatever topics I’m currently fascinated with as well as a portfolio of some of my personal projects.
